THE ROAD NOT TAKEN

BY ROBERT FROST

                           Two roads
                         diverged in a
                      yellow wood and
                  sorry I could not travel
              both and be one traveler,long
            I stood And looked down one as
   far as I could to where it bent in the undergrowth
      then took the other, as just as fair, and having
           perhaps the better claim, because it was
               grassy and wanted wear though
                 as for that the passing there
                     had worn them really
                         about the same,
